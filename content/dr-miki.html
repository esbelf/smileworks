Get to Know Dr. Miki

Dr. Miki says that the best thing about her job is being able to provide truly personalized care for each patient. She loves taking the time to listen to their concerns, understand their needs, and work together to create treatment plans that focus on their long-term health and well-being. Building strong relationships with her patients and seeing their confidence grow through their smiles is incredibly rewarding.

Why Did You Decide to Become a Dentist?

Dr. Miki knew she wanted to become a dentist during high school after undergoing braces, which dramatically changed her smile and boosted her self-confidence. That experience, combined with volunteering at a dental clinic, showed her how transformative dental care can be. She was drawn to the mix of science, hands-on work, and the opportunity to help others feel better about their teeth and overall health.

Where Did You Study Dentistry?

A proud alumnus of Texas A&M and the Boston University Henry M. Goldman School of Dental Medicine, Dr. Miki has always believed that dentistry is a blend of art and science, dedicating herself to continually learning and improving so she can give her patients the best possible care.

With a Fellowship in the Academy of General Dentistry—a distinction only 6% of dentists achieve—Dr. Miki has completed hundreds of hours of continuing education across a wide range of dental fields. She’s a member of the Academy of Cosmetic Dentistry and certified in Botox and Invisalign, which allows her to offer a comprehensive approach to enhancing both the health and aesthetics of the smile.

On top of this, she has taken advanced training through the Kois Center, Seattle Study Club, Dr. Apa Courses, and is an active member of the South Carolina Dental Association.

Outside of the Dental Office, What Do You Like to Do?

When not brightening smiles, you can find Dr. Miki engrossed in a good book, sipping on hot tea, or attending conferences and seminars to stay at the forefront of dental advancements.
