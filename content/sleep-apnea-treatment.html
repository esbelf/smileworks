What Is Sleep Apnea?

Sleep apnea is a disorder that stops your breathing while asleep, preventing proper nighttime rest. This stoppage tends to result in symptoms like loud snoring, insomnia, daytime fatigue, and irritability. When it's left unchecked, it can even cause severe health complications.

There are two main types of sleep apnea, each with its own causes. The more common one is obstructive sleep apnea (OSA), which occurs when throat muscles block your airway. Meanwhile, central sleep apnea (CSA) happens when your brain doesn’t send the signals needed to breathe.
